Quote Validation Report — Quote #4133

SUNSHINE COAST AIRPORT

Executive Verdict

This quote appears broadly competitive on total sell price for a project of this type, but it is priced very tightly. Most line items look fair to slightly low for the Australian electrical/lighting wholesale market, with the downlights in particular looking aggressively priced. The main concern is not customer overpricing, but supplier under-recovery of margin given the scope, assembly requirements, and project complexity.

Margin Check

The quoted margin of 15.3% is below the typical 25–35% gross profit range often targeted by Australian wholesale/service businesses for sustainable project work. That does not automatically make the quote wrong, but it does indicate a thin buffer for freight variation, supplier increases, breakage, warranty exposure, or scope creep. From a commercial standpoint, this is a low-margin job rather than a healthy-margin one.

Risk Flags

  • Downlight unit rate at $7.70 appears materially under market for the stated specification.
  • LED strip rate may be too low if it truly includes extrusion, tails, drivers, and assembly into channel.
  • L1/L2 is quoted as one lot “as per Hawko breakdown”; if that reference is not attached, scope ambiguity remains.
  • 15.3% margin leaves limited protection against freight, supplier cost movement, defects, or project changes.
  • F.I.S. via general road freight should be checked against packaging/protection needs for suspended extrusion products.

Recommendation

Adjust specific lines before issuing — particularly review the D1 downlight pricing and confirm the full inclusions on the LED strip/extrusion line. If strategic pricing is required to win the job, proceed only with explicit internal approval that this is a low-margin quote.
